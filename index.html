Our Support Services

Brother Bears is a not for profit organisation that aims to support young children who experience the loss of a sibling. Our packages are designed to provide some comfort to siblings, help with memory creation and assist them with understanding what has happened.

Sibling Sacks

Our Sibling Sacks are designed to help young children with understanding what has happened, create memories and provide comfort. The standard pack contains a suitable book & soft toy in a reusable bag.

We have 3 book options available for our packs to best suit the child's circumstances.

Something Happened

Something Happened is a storybook explaining Stillbirth and contains notes for carers to help them guide the grieving sibling

Yesterday You Were Here

Yesterday You Were Here is a beautifully written book for children aged 3-6 who loose a sibling or friend the same age

*This book is also available to schools.

Remembering Me: Sibling Edition

Remembering Me: Sibling Edition provides a space for children to write memories and connect with their sibling.

Other Projects

Cuddle Cots

We have donated CuddleCot packages to Beaudesert Hostpital in Queensland & Canberra's Calvary Bruce Hospital. Cuddle Cots allow families to spend more time with their baby and make precious memories before saying goodbye.

Support Boxes

Sibling support boxes are Individually designed to help a child with capturing memories, providing comfort and assisting with grieving after the sudden death of a sibling. Memory boxes contain items to help parents create precious memories with their baby soon after birth.

About Us

Brother Bears was founded in memory of Shay James Dowling who was stillborn in October 2016.

His older brother was only 11 months old at the time of Shay’s birth and the “Brother Bears” that this organisation was named after represent the brothers.

His Mum, Katie, wanted to do something to support siblings as she realised that they are often the forgotten ones when a baby or child dies. She settled on creating “Sibling Sacks” and in early 2017 began to develop the idea further. The first Sibling Sack was delivered in late 2017.

With the support of family, friends and the community Brother Bears donated a cuddlecot & memory boxes to Beaudesert Hospital in February 2017 and then delivered their second CuddleCot package in conjunction with the Willis family to Canberra’s Calvary Hospital in the October. Since then Brother Bears has mainly focused on the Sibling Sack project.

Katie is proud in knowing that the sibling support packages are helping bereaved siblings throughout Australia and thanks everyone who helps keep this project going.
